⭐ Today's Lead · Nation

In a 5–4 ruling, the Supreme Court blocks Trump from firing Fed Governor Lisa Cook — a landmark win for central-bank independence

The Court handed down a term-defining decision Monday: President Trump cannot, for now, remove Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook, who last year became the first Fed governor fired in the central bank's 111-year history. Chief Justice John Roberts and Justice Brett Kavanaugh joined the three liberal justices to form a 5–4 majority, with Roberts writing that accepting the government's view would "transform the Federal Reserve's for-cause protection into at-will employment — an interpretive leap out of step with… our Nation's tradition of central banking protected from political interference." Cook stays on the job while her challenge proceeds. Markets read it as a green light for Fed independence and rallied hard — the Nasdaq closing up about 1.8%. The Court saved its other blockbusters, including birthright citizenship, for Tuesday. Finish reading at NOTUS →
