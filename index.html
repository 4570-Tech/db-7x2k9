⭐ Today's Lead · Markets & Space

SpaceX rockets onto the Nasdaq 100 — and Elon Musk becomes the world's first trillionaire

Nasdaq said Friday that SpaceX will join the Nasdaq-100 before the open on July 7, one of the fastest index inclusions ever under a newly adopted 15-trading-day fast-track. It caps a wild three weeks: the June 12 IPO — the largest in history — priced at $135, opened at $150, and closed near $161, valuing SpaceX around $2.1 trillion and tipping Musk's net worth past $1 trillion on paper. Now the mechanics get interesting: index funds tracking the Nasdaq-100 are forced to buy, teeing up a wave of passive demand. The detail the celebration skips — SpaceX posted a $4.9 billion net loss last year, so trillions in value ride on Starship and Starlink delivering, not current profits. Finish reading at CNBC →
