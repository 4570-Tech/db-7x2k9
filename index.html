⭐ Today's Lead · World

A small plane slams into Beijing's tallest skyscraper — then China makes it disappear

Just before 6 p.m. local time Friday, a single-engine Sunward SA60L light plane flew into the CITIC Tower (“China Zun”), Beijing's tallest building, raining debris onto crowds below. Officials say the pilot was killed and 13 people on the ground and inside were injured; flight data shows the aircraft took off from an airfield east of the city and veered off a normal training route toward downtown. The strangest part came after: by Saturday, videos, photos and search results were being scrubbed from the Chinese internet, and the area looked, in CNN's words, “like nothing had happened.” Authorities haven't said whether it was an accident, a medical emergency, or something deliberate — which is exactly the vacuum censorship tends to fill with rumor.
