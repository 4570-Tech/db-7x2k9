📉 Social Security trust fund now projected to run dry in late 2032🆓 Free

The 2026 Trustees Report (released June 9) moved the depletion date up a quarter to late 2032 — about six years out. If Congress does nothing, incoming payroll taxes would then cover only ~78% of scheduled benefits, an automatic ~22% cut. Not a reason to claim early in a panic, but a real nudge to stress-test your retirement plan against a haircut scenario and keep an eye on reform talk.

Center for Retirement Research · Kiplinger · 401k SpecialistJun 9
💼 Big 401(k) & IRA changes land for 2026 — including a Roth catch-up shift🆓 Free

Savers get more room in 2026: the 401(k) cap rises to $24,500 ($8,000 catch-up at 50+), and the IRA limit goes to $7,500 ($1,100 catch-up). The wrinkle worth planning around: higher earners' catch-up contributions must now go Roth (after-tax) — a meaningful tax shift if you're 50+ and used to the upfront deduction. Worth a quick look at your contribution elections before year-end.
