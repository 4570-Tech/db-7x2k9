⭐ Today's Lead · World

A two-week-old US–Iran ceasefire teeters as Tehran strikes Gulf bases and Trump warns Iran "will no longer exist"

The fragile understanding signed earlier this month is suddenly looking very fragile indeed. Over the weekend the US struck Iranian sites; in response, Iran's Revolutionary Guard said it targeted American military facilities in Bahrain and Kuwait, with a residential building in Bahrain's Muharraq governorate heavily damaged overnight. President Trump warned that Iran "will no longer exist" if the strikes continue, while Tehran threatened a "complete halt" to negotiations. For all the brinkmanship, both sides also signaled they'll stand down for now and meet again in Doha on June 30 — and with tankers already returning to the Strait of Hormuz (a fifth of the world's oil), markets are betting cooler heads prevail.
