⚖️ SCOTUS hands Trump a TPS win — courts can't second-guess most terminations🆕 New🆓 Free

In Mullin v. Doe (consolidated with Trump v. Miot), the Court ruled 6–3 Thursday that the TPS statute bars courts from reviewing most challenges to ending a country's designation — lifting lower-court holds that had paused terminations for an estimated 350,000 Haitians and ~6,000 Syrians. Justice Alito wrote for the majority; the Haitian plaintiffs' race-discrimination claim was deemed unlikely to succeed, though the Syria-case claim survives. Still pending before recess: the marquee birthright-citizenship case (Trump v. Barbara), expected by early July.
