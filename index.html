The Dow closes above 52,000 for the first time as a tech-led rally sends Wall Street to records

Wall Street roared into the history books Monday: the Dow Jones Industrial Average closed above 52,000 for the first time ever, while the S&P 500 rose about 1.2% and the tech-heavy Nasdaq jumped roughly 2%, powered by Alphabet and the rest of the megacap-tech crowd. The spark: relief that the Supreme Court left Fed Governor Lisa Cook in place, plus a US–Iran stand-down that cooled oil. Futures point modestly higher again this morning. But the celebration faces a test — the all-important June jobs report lands Thursday (markets are closed Friday for the Fourth), the 10-year Treasury still yields about 4.38%, and a freshly widened goods-trade gap is a reminder the backdrop isn't all blue sky. 🛰️ NASA launches a first-of-its-kind robot rescue today to save its falling Swift telescope🆕 New🆓 Free

In a daring $30 million first, a robot spacecraft called LINK — built by Arizona's Katalyst Space — launches today on the final-ever Pegasus XL to grapple NASA's aging Swift observatory (no engines of its own) and tow it to a safe orbit before it falls into the atmosphere by October.
