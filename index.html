🏛️ Supreme Court Hands Trump Two Landmark Immigration Victories
NEW
July 1, 2026

The Supreme Court issued a pair of sweeping immigration rulings Tuesday that green-light two of the administration's most aggressive enforcement moves. In the first, a 6-3 majority allowed the White House to end Temporary Protected Status for hundreds of thousands of Haitians and Syrians — clearing the path to deportation for a population that has lived and worked legally in the U.S. for years. The court said a provision in the TPS statute bars courts from reviewing the Secretary's designation decisions entirely, handing the executive branch nearly unchecked authority over the program.

In the second ruling, the Court blessed the administration's border "metering" practice — capping how many asylum seekers can present themselves at ports of entry each day. Writing for the majority, the Court drew a line between migrants who physically step onto U.S. soil (who retain the right to seek asylum) and those turned back before crossing, arguing the statute requires one to "arrive in" rather than merely "arrive at" the country. The dissenting justices called it a "word-game" that guts asylum law in practice.
